About

17 years of design — across music, enterprise, and the AI frontier.

I started in the creative industry — designing for Warner Music Group, building digital experiences for Taco Bell and American Express. From there I moved into product: seven years at Google Cloud, progressing from IC to design manager, leading UX for a portfolio responsible for two-thirds of the organization's revenue.

Most recently I built HEARD — an AI music generator for lyricists — from zero to a live product with real users. I identified the gap, designed the experience, coded the stack, and shipped it. That's how I work: end-to-end, with mastery of the craft and genuine care for the people on the team and the people using what we make.

I'm most energized where the technology is new, the team is curious, and the work expands what's possible.

My Why
Why design?

Because design is the practice of making things accessible — not just usable, but genuinely within reach for the people who need them most. The best work I've done has always lived at that gap: between what a technology promises and what a real person can actually experience.

I was drawn to this field because I believe tools should expand people's capabilities, not require expertise to operate. That conviction has followed me from consumer apps to enterprise infrastructure to AI products. The domain changes. The question stays the same: who's being left out, and how do we close that distance?

I lead with that question, and I build teams that ask it too.

How I structure teams
Clear ownership
Each designer owns a product area end-to-end. Accountability creates confidence, not pressure.
T-shaped craft
Strong generalists with depth in one area — research, systems, interaction. I hire for range and grow for depth.
Embedded collaboration
Designers sit inside xfn pods (PM, Eng, QA) so context doesn't get lost in handoffs.
